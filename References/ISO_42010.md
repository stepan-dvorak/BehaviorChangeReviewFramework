# ISO_42010.md

# Purpose

This note evaluates **ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010:2022 — Software, systems and enterprise — Architecture description** as a foundation for the Behavior Change Review Framework project.

The objective is to determine:

1. which terminology and concepts can be reused;
2. whether the standard already defines the proposed review approach;
3. whether behavior-changing customizations can be represented using its conceptual model;
4. where the standard's scope ends and where additional Business Central guidance would still be necessary.

This is an analytical research note, not a substitute for the licensed standard.

# Source Status

ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010:2022 is the second edition of the international architecture-description standard. It was published in November 2022 and superseded the 2011 edition.

The standard applies to architecture descriptions for a broad range of entities, including:

- software;
- systems;
- enterprises;
- systems of systems;
- products and product lines;
- services and service lines;
- technologies;
- business domains.

Official source:

- [ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010:2022 — ISO](https://www.iso.org/standard/74393.html)
- [IEEE/ISO/IEC 42010-2022 — IEEE Standards Association](https://standards.ieee.org/ieee/42010/6846/)

# Executive Assessment

## What ISO 42010 provides

ISO 42010 provides a rigorous conceptual and documentary foundation for:

- identifying an **entity of interest**;
- distinguishing its architecture from an **architecture description**;
- identifying stakeholders and their concerns;
- organizing concerns into stakeholder perspectives;
- defining architecture viewpoints;
- constructing architecture views and models;
- recording correspondences and inconsistencies;
- recording architecture decisions and rationale;
- defining architecture description frameworks and languages.

## What ISO 42010 does not provide

The standard explicitly does **not** prescribe:

- an architecting process;
- an architecture review process;
- a behavior-change methodology;
- implementation techniques;
- development tools;
- specific notations;
- criteria for deciding when a code change requires architecture review.

Therefore:

> ISO 42010 can structure the description of a behavior-changing customization and its consequences, but it does not itself define the proposed Behavior Change Review Trigger or review workflow.

## Relevance to the project

The standard strongly supports the project's claim that architectural consequences should be considered through multiple stakeholder concerns and views rather than reduced to one implementation mechanism.

However, it does not state that modification of an established effective execution flow is itself a mandatory review trigger.

The project's possible contribution remains an **operational specialization and integration** of existing architecture concepts for Business Central.

# Scope Boundary of the Standard

A critical distinction in ISO 42010 is the separation between:

- the **architecture** of the entity of interest;
- the **architecture description** that expresses that architecture.

The standard defines requirements for the structure and expression of the architecture description. It does not define the architecture of a particular entity and does not impose requirements on the entity itself.

This matters to the project because the proposed framework should not claim:

> ISO 42010 requires every behavior-changing customization to preserve specific architectural properties.

A more defensible statement is:

> ISO 42010 provides concepts through which the affected properties, stakeholders, concerns, decisions, views and inconsistencies of a behavior-changing customization can be explicitly described and reviewed.

# Core Concepts Relevant to the Project

## Entity of Interest

The **entity of interest** is the subject whose architecture is being described.

It can be broader or narrower than an entire software product.

For the Business Central project, possible entities of interest include:

- a complete Business Central solution;
- an extension ecosystem;
- a posting subsystem;
- a localization application;
- a document-processing capability;
- one established extensibility surface;
- one behavior-changing customization and its host context.

### Research implication

The project does not need to treat the entire Business Central platform as the entity of interest for every review.

A review can define a deliberately bounded entity, for example:

> Czech fixed-asset disposal posting as implemented by the Base Application and localization extension.

This addresses an important scope problem in [[01_Terminology]]: the affected execution flow has no useful meaning until the entity and boundary of analysis are identified.

## Architecture

ISO 42010 distinguishes architecture from its description.

For this project, that distinction discourages treating one flow diagram, call graph or source listing as “the architecture”.

A behavior-changing customization can affect the architecture even when the architecture description has not been updated. Conversely, documentation can describe intended behavior that no longer corresponds to the effective runtime system.

### Research implication

The project should distinguish:

- **architectural reality** — the effective organization and relationships present in the deployed solution;
- **architecture description** — the explicit artifact used to communicate and review it.

This reinforces the value of a review record without implying that the record itself is the changed architecture.

## Architecture Description

An architecture description is a work product used to express an architecture.

It can contain multiple views, models, decisions, rationale, stakeholders, concerns and correspondences.

### Research implication

A future Behavior Change Impact Record could be treated as:

- a focused architecture-description artifact; or
- a component of a larger architecture description.

This is preferable to presenting the checklist as an isolated coding convention.

## Stakeholder

A stakeholder is a party with interests in the entity of interest.

Relevant Business Central stakeholders may include:

- customer or product owner;
- business process owner;
- implementation partner;
- extension developer;
- maintainer;
- upgrade team;
- security or compliance owner;
- operations and support;
- another extension publisher;
- Microsoft platform or localization team.

### Research implication

The effects of a behavior change cannot be evaluated only from the perspective of the developer implementing it.

For example:

- the developer may care about immediate functional correctness;
- another extension publisher may care about extension-point semantics;
- operations may care about telemetry;
- the upgrade team may care about compatibility with future Base Application changes;
- accounting stakeholders may care about posting invariants and auditability.

This gives a standards-based foundation for the claim that visible functional output alone is insufficient.

## Concern

A concern is an interest relevant to one or more stakeholders.

For the proposed framework, concerns may include:

- correct business outcome;
- data integrity;
- transaction atomicity;
- authorization;
- auditability;
- extensibility;
- effective extensibility;
- error and retry semantics;
- performance;
- maintainability;
- upgradeability;
- compatibility;
- observability;
- dependency direction;
- release ownership.

### Research implication

The phrase used earlier in the project — “architectural properties carried by the flow” — is intuitive but potentially misleading.

ISO 42010 suggests a more precise formulation:

> A behavior-changing customization may affect stakeholder concerns that were previously addressed by the established architecture and its effective execution flow.

This avoids implying that a flow literally “contains” quality attributes as intrinsic objects.

### Recommended terminology refinement

Current phrase:

> architectural properties carried by the flow

Potential ISO-aligned replacement:

> stakeholder concerns and architectural decisions affected by the established flow

or:

> architectural concerns addressed by the affected behavior

The original phrase can remain explanatory language, but should not become a formal definition without further evidence.

## Stakeholder Perspective

The 2022 edition introduces stakeholder perspectives as a means of grouping concerns and organizing viewpoints that frame those concerns.

Potential project perspectives could include:

- functional correctness;
- data integrity and accounting;
- extensibility and ecosystem composition;
- security and compliance;
- runtime and transaction behavior;
- operations and observability;
- evolution and upgrade compatibility.

### Research implication

The review checklist may eventually be organized by perspectives rather than as one undifferentiated list.

This may improve usability:

```text
Behavior Change Review
├── Functional perspective
├── Integrity perspective
├── Runtime perspective
├── Extensibility perspective
├── Operational perspective
└── Evolution perspective
```

This would be strongly aligned with ISO 42010 without claiming that the standard defines these specific Business Central perspectives.

## Architecture Viewpoint

An architecture viewpoint specifies conventions for constructing, interpreting and using a view to frame particular concerns.

A viewpoint is not the same thing as a view.

A reusable behavior-change viewpoint could specify:

- stakeholders;
- concerns;
- model kinds;
- notation;
- analysis techniques;
- consistency rules;
- expected outcomes of the review.

### Research implication

The strongest possible ISO-aligned output of the project may not be a “new architectural entity” or even only a checklist.

It may be:

> a Business Central Behavior Change Architecture Viewpoint

Such a viewpoint could define how to construct a focused architecture view for a significant behavior-changing customization.

This is currently a **candidate direction**, not an accepted conclusion.

### Candidate viewpoint scope

A Behavior Change Viewpoint could frame concerns such as:

- what established behavior is altered;
- how control or responsibility changes;
- which outcomes and invariants are preserved or redefined;
- which extension capabilities remain effective;
- which transaction and failure semantics change;
- which architecture decisions are introduced;
- how the customization evolves with future upstream changes.

## Architecture View

An architecture view expresses the architecture from the perspective of particular concerns, conforming to a viewpoint.

For the project, a concrete review document for one customization could be a behavior-change architecture view.

Example:

> Architecture view: Replacement of fixed-asset disposal posting by Czech localization.

It could contain:

- boundary and entity of interest;
- original execution structure;
- modified effective execution structure;
- affected stakeholders;
- affected concerns;
- changed decisions;
- preserved and redefined contracts;
- unresolved risks and inconsistencies.

### Research implication

This concept provides a stronger formal basis than calling the execution flow itself a first-class architectural asset.

The **flow representation** may be one model inside an architecture view. The complete review artifact addresses more than the flow.

## Model and Model Kind

Architecture views are composed using architecture models. Model kinds define conventions for particular classes of models.

Potential model kinds for the project include:

- execution-flow model;
- event-participation model;
- transaction-boundary model;
- responsibility-allocation model;
- dependency model;
- state-transition model;
- before-and-after correspondence model.

### Research implication

“Execution Flow” does not need to become the umbrella architecture concept.

It can be treated as one model kind used to express the behavioral consequences of a customization.

This directly corrects an earlier research direction recorded in [[00_Research_Log]].

## Architecture Decision and Rationale

ISO 42010 supports recording architecture decisions and their rationale.

A behavior-changing customization often embodies decisions such as:

- suppress the standard continuation;
- delegate calculation to an interface;
- replace posting logic for a localization;
- expose or omit replacement extension points;
- preserve or redefine event semantics;
- accept dependency on upstream internal behavior.

### Research implication

The proposed framework should capture not only effects, but also:

- alternatives considered;
- reasons for selecting the mechanism;
- reasons for changing behavior;
- accepted consequences;
- rejected alternatives;
- conditions under which the decision should be revisited.

This supports the project's planned Research Log and future impact-record template.

## Correspondence

Correspondences relate elements in an architecture description.

For behavior changes, useful correspondences include:

- original step ↔ replacement step;
- original event ↔ replacement event;
- original invariant ↔ replacement validation;
- original transaction boundary ↔ new transaction boundary;
- stakeholder concern ↔ model or decision addressing it;
- original observable outcome ↔ modified outcome.

### Research implication

This concept is especially valuable for distinguishing:

- simple technical reachability;
- semantic preservation;
- equivalent replacement;
- intentional incompatibility.

A replacement event having the same timing or name is not sufficient. A correspondence must explain whether it addresses the same concern and preserves the same semantics.

## Correspondence Rules

Correspondence rules define expectations governing relationships between elements.

Potential project rules could include:

- every suppressed externally supported extension capability must have a documented disposition;
- every intentionally changed invariant must map to an explicit architecture decision;
- every new execution branch must map to defined outcomes and failure behavior;
- every claimed equivalent extension point must document semantic correspondence, not only technical invocation.

These rules would be project-specific specializations, not requirements quoted from ISO 42010.

## Inconsistency

An architecture description can identify inconsistencies among its elements rather than pretending all views are automatically coherent.

For this project, examples include:

- an event remains publicly available but is no longer behaviorally effective;
- documentation states that a standard flow is preserved while an extension suppresses it;
- a replacement claims equivalent behavior but changes transaction semantics;
- an interface implementation meets the type contract but omits required domain events;
- the behavior view and extension-capability view disagree.

### Research implication

“Inconsistency” may be a more precise term than “broken architecture” in many review findings.

The review framework should allow a change to be accepted with documented inconsistency or risk instead of assuming every difference is forbidden.

## Architecture Description Framework

An architecture description framework establishes conventions and practices for creating architecture descriptions in a particular domain or community.

### Research implication

The long-term project could evolve into:

> a lightweight Business Central Architecture Description Framework for Behavior-Changing Customizations.

However, that may be too ambitious for the first whitepaper.

A more realistic progression is:

1. review guideline;
2. reusable viewpoint;
3. example views;
4. only later, a broader architecture description framework.

# Does ISO 42010 Already Solve the Research Problem?

## Yes, partially

It already provides:

- a rigorous language for identifying stakeholders and concerns;
- a method-independent structure for describing architecture;
- viewpoints and views for separating concerns;
- architecture decisions and rationale;
- correspondences and inconsistencies;
- a framework for reusable description conventions.

Therefore, the project should not claim that software architecture lacks a way to describe the consequences of behavior changes.

## No, not operationally

ISO 42010 does not specify:

- when a source-code customization becomes architecture-significant;
- when review must be triggered;
- how Business Central behavior should be analyzed;
- which Business Central concerns are mandatory;
- how to inspect a suppressed continuation;
- how to evaluate effective extensibility;
- which alternatives to `IsHandled` should be selected;
- how to apply the method in a pull request.

Therefore, the project's practical contribution can remain valid.

# Important Correction to the Working Hypothesis

Current wording in the project:

> A significant change to an established effective execution flow may also modify architectural properties carried by that flow.

ISO-aligned wording:

> A significant change to established behavior may affect stakeholder concerns, architecture decisions, correspondences and inconsistencies associated with the existing architecture and its effective execution.

This wording is less intuitive but conceptually safer.

A practical compromise:

> A significant change to an established effective execution flow may affect architectural concerns previously addressed by that flow. Those impacts should be reviewed independently of the mechanism used to introduce the change.

This should be considered for [[01_Terminology]] and the whitepaper.

# Candidate Project Contribution After ISO 42010 Review

The strongest current interpretation is:

> The project can define a lightweight, mechanism-independent Business Central behavior-change viewpoint and review procedure, aligned with ISO/IEC/IEEE 42010 concepts.

Its contribution would include:

- identifying the review trigger;
- defining Business Central stakeholders and concerns;
- defining required models;
- defining correspondences between original and modified behavior;
- documenting decisions and inconsistencies;
- supplying examples and practical implementation alternatives.

This is narrower and more defensible than claiming a new general architectural theory.

# Relationship to ISO/IEC/IEEE 42020 and 42030

ISO 42010 is only one member of a larger family.

Official IEEE descriptions indicate:

- **ISO/IEC/IEEE 42020:2019** defines architecture processes, including governance, management, conceptualization, evaluation and elaboration;
- **ISO/IEC/IEEE 42030:2019** defines an architecture evaluation framework, including suitability assessment, comparison of alternatives and assessment of impact from a change to an established architecture.

These standards may be more directly relevant to the review process and trigger question than ISO 42010.

Therefore:

> No conclusion about the novelty of Behavioral Change Review should be accepted until ISO 42020 and ISO 42030 have been investigated.

New reference notes should be added:

- `ISO_42020.md`
- `ISO_42030.md`

- Microsoft localization team;
- implementation partner;
- customer;

- support and upgrade teams;
- auditors or accounting stakeholders.

## Concerns

- statutory correctness;
- ledger integrity;
- transaction semantics;
- supported integration events;
- effective extensibility;
- upgrade compatibility;
- diagnostics.

## Views and models

- original execution-flow model;
- modified effective-flow model;
- event-participation model;
- transaction model;
- responsibility allocation;
- correspondence table.

## Decisions

- why the base flow is replaced;
- why a given mechanism is used;
- which standard capabilities are intentionally preserved or omitted.

## Inconsistencies and risks

- events still exist but do not affect the replacement flow;
- copied logic may diverge from future base changes;
- replacement behavior may satisfy localization concerns while weakening ecosystem extensibility.

# Counterarguments and Limitations

## ISO 42010 is descriptive, not prescriptive

Using its concepts does not prove that every behavior change must receive formal architecture review.

The trigger remains a project hypothesis.

## Architecture descriptions can become too heavy

A full standards-conformant AD would be disproportionate for most pull requests.

The project must produce a lightweight specialization.

## Concerns are context-dependent

There is no universal list of properties that every replacement must preserve.

The framework should identify and review relevant concerns, not impose identical behavior.

## Behavior may be represented without a dedicated flow model

State models, scenarios, interactions or decision models may sometimes be more appropriate than an execution-flow representation.

Therefore, the project must not require “Execution Flow” as the only model kind.

## Architectural significance remains unresolved

ISO 42010 helps describe an architecture once the entity and concerns are chosen. It does not supply a simple threshold distinguishing local implementation changes from architecture-significant behavior changes.

# Findings

## Finding 1 — Accepted

System behavior and behavior-related concerns can be legitimate content of architecture descriptions.

## Finding 2 — Accepted

The project should frame architectural consequences as stakeholder concerns rather than vaguely treating them as properties literally carried by a flow.

## Finding 3 — Accepted

Execution Flow is best treated provisionally as an architecture model or model kind, not as the umbrella architectural entity.

## Finding 4 — Candidate

The proposed review framework may be expressed as a reusable architecture viewpoint.

## Finding 5 — Accepted

ISO 42010 does not define the operational review trigger sought by this project.

## Finding 6 — Required next research

ISO 42020 and ISO 42030 must be analyzed before making originality claims concerning architecture processes or evaluation.

# Open Questions

1. Can a lightweight Behavior Change Viewpoint be made conformant in spirit without claiming formal ISO conformance?
2. Which concerns are specific enough to Business Central to be mandatory?
3. Is a single viewpoint sufficient, or are multiple perspectives/viewpoints needed?
4. Should Effective Extensibility be a concern, a measured quality, or a derived assessment?
